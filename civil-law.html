Protecting rights and resolving disputes with fairness and expertise

Civil law refers to the body of laws that govern private disputes between individuals or organizations, excluding criminal law and administrative law.

It encompasses a broad spectrum of legal issues such as contracts, property disputes, family law matters like divorce and child custody, torts (personal injury claims), and inheritance issues. Civil law aims to resolve conflicts and provide remedies, typically through monetary compensation or specific performance, based on established legal principles and statutes. Its primary goal is to uphold justice and fairness in relationships between private parties within a civil society.
